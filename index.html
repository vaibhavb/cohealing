About

As humanity undergoes a transformation, we are each being challenged to move beyond outdated, limiting beliefs and behaviors in order to evolve and achieve a new version of ourselves and our collective world. Cohealing is a community meditation group, where we are singularly focused on exploring well-being and making self-care practices accessible to all, especially within communities where the need is greatest (which is now!). Cohealing is an enabler. This meditation & restoration experience aims to show you how, by directing your attention within, you can build resilience, find healing, and create a new version of your life and our shared world. We are grateful to have you as part of our meditation community

Classes

We intend to offer a restorative 10 week program to help participants in these troubled times. The outline themes for the sessions are below and the meditations will be supplemented by various practices like restorative yoga, sound healing, habit tracking etal. We plan to do sessions on Tuesdays and Thursday every week online from 6 - 6:30pm PST. Here’s is our 10 week program with themes outlined:
  • Week 1 - Reconnecting with your mind & body
  • Week 2 - Positive Thinking
  • Week 3 - Pain Management
  • Week 4 - Anger Management
  • Week 5 - Dealing with Fear & Anxiety
  • Week 6 - Acceptance & Self Care
  • Week 7 - Building Resilience
  • Week 8 - Sleep Management
  • Week 9 - Patience
  • Week 10 - Stress Management

Coaches

In early 2020, I was dealing with chronic pain and felt immense stress and the string of events following added a whole lot to it. I created a self healing plan that worked for me and got me to a better place today. A succession of difficult events led me to cogitate a lot, introducing me to meditation. With consistent meditation, I became more mindful. I’m learning the art of being present, less reactive, which overall helped me maintain my sanity in the toughest moments. I learnt to build necessary routines involving physical exercise and meditation to stay healthy, and mindful. I intend to practice those habits, and make them second nature rather than conscious endeavors. As Aristotle stated, “Excellence is not an act, but a habit”.
